How the Strategy Works

Step 1: Identify Conviction Buys

When a 13F filing is released, we compare share counts to the previous quarter. We select the top 20 stocks where NPS increased shares the most (by percentage). This filters for high-conviction buys.

Step 2: Equal-Weight Portfolio

We buy all 20 stocks in equal weight on the first business day after the filing date. No position sizing optimization — simple 5% allocation to each stock.

Step 3: Hold Until Next Filing

We hold the portfolio until the next 13F filing date (roughly 3 months). Then we rebalance to the new top 20. This creates roughly 4 rebalances per year.

Why This Might Work

Informed Signal

NPS employs hundreds of analysts and external advisors. When they significantly increase a position, it likely reflects deep research and institutional conviction that individual investors rarely have access to.

Delayed Information Advantage

While 13F filings are delayed by 45 days, the institutional thesis behind a large share increase often plays out over quarters, not days. The signal remains actionable even with the delay.

Behavioral Edge

Large pension funds like NPS tend to avoid panic selling and have multi-year investment horizons. Their increases often align with long-term value creation rather than short-term momentum.
