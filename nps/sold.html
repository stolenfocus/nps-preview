Why Did NPS Sell?

M&A Completion

The largest exit, Kellanova ($41.6M), was driven by Mars Inc's acquisition. When a company is acquired, the shares are cashed out and automatically disappear from the next 13F filing. This is not a bearish signal — it's simply the deal closing.

Small Position Cleanup

Many of these exits (FactSet, Align, Camden, Pool Corp) were very small positions under $500K — likely remnants being cleaned up rather than high-conviction sells. Institutional funds regularly prune sub-threshold holdings.

Sector Rotation

The exit from Owens Corning ($13.5M) while simultaneously buying CRH ($258M) suggests NPS is rotating within building materials — preferring CRH's global scale and infrastructure exposure over Owens Corning's more residential-focused business.

Important context: 13F filings show positions at quarter-end. NPS may have sold these stocks at any point during Q4 2025 (October through December). The actual sale prices are unknown.
